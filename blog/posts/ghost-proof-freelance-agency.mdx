# The Ghost-Proof Agency: Why Proposals are Only 50% of the Deal

In the freelance world, we often celebrate the moment a client says "Yes" to a proposal. We pop the champagne, we update our spreadsheets, and we start the work.

But there is a silent crisis happening in the global freelance economy: **Ghosting.**

According to recent data, 47% of freelancers have experienced a situation where a client pays the initial deposit, then completely disappears during the project—leaving the freelancer with half-finished work and zero recourse.

## The Flaw in the "Proposal-Only" Model

Most freelancers treat the proposal as the end of the sales process. They use tools like PandaDoc or BetterProposals to send a "pretty" document, and they think the job is done.

But a proposal is just a pitch. It’s a promise. It’s not a **Legal Shield.**

If you are just sending proposals, you are only doing 50% of the deal. The other 50% is **Securing the Revenue.**

## The A-to-Z Workflow: Your Defensive Strategy

To build a "Ghost-Proof" agency, you need to transition from a document-based workflow to a **Lifecycle workflow.**

Here is the Proposar A-to-Z model that high-ticket agencies are using to protect their cash flow:

### 1. The Strategy (AI Draft)
Don't just sell services; sell a strategy. Use AI to generate a proposal that focuses on the client's ROI, not just your billable hours.

### 2. Universal Delivery (The Multi-Channel Edge)
Stop relying on email attachments that get buried in spam. Share your proposal via a secure link, and follow up directly on **WhatsApp.** The open rates for WhatsApp are 98%—making it nearly impossible for a client to "forget" your deal.

### 3. The Smart Contract (The Anchor)
This is where most freelancers fail. They wait days between the proposal acceptance and the contract signing. With Proposar, the moment a client clicks "Accept," a legal-grade contract is generated from the same data. No friction. No handshakes. Just a binding agreement.

### 4. IP Locking & Kill-Fees
Your contract should explicitly state that the **Intellectual Property (IP)** only transfers to the client after the **Final Invoice** is paid. This is your leverage. If they ghost you, they don't own the work.
